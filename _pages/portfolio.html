The ultimate aim of the modern movement in biology is to explain all biology in terms of physics and chemistry –Francis Crick

The most striking feature of biological cells is that they are overcrowded with diverse biomacromolecules.

For instance, approximately 34-44% of E. coli cytoplasm volume is occupied by macromolecules, which makes it crowded to a similar extent to Times Square on New Year's Eve. Such crowding impacts the physical chemistry of the intracellular milieu in a complex way. Apart from macromolecular crowding, there are also other interesting features of cellular compartments. In particular, a cell is a non-equilibrium system, and changes in metabolic activity reportedly glassificate its cytoplasm.
